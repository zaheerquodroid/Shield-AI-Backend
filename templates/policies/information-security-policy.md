## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the framework for managing information security at {{COMPANY_NAME}}. It defines the principles, responsibilities, and controls necessary to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All employees, contractors, and third-party personnel of {{COMPANY_NAME}}
- All information systems, networks, and data owned or managed by {{COMPANY_NAME}}
- All locations where {{COMPANY_NAME}} information is processed, stored, or transmitted

## 3. Policy Statement

{{COMPANY_NAME}} is committed to protecting its information assets against unauthorized access, disclosure, modification, destruction, and interference. Information security is the responsibility of every individual who accesses {{COMPANY_NAME}} systems and data.

## 4. Information Security Principles

### 4.1 Confidentiality

Information shall be accessible only to those authorized to have access. Access is granted on a need-to-know basis and enforced through role-based access controls.

### 4.2 Integrity

Information shall be accurate, complete, and protected from unauthorized modification. Change management processes shall ensure controlled modifications to systems and data.

### 4.3 Availability

Information and associated systems shall be available when needed. Business continuity and disaster recovery plans shall ensure service resilience.

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities

### 5.1 Executive Leadership

- Approve information security policies and provide strategic direction
- Allocate adequate resources for information security initiatives
- Review security posture quarterly

### 5.2 Policy Owner ({{POLICY_OWNER}})

- Maintain and update this policy on an annual basis
- Coordinate security awareness training programs
- Report security metrics to executive leadership

### 5.3 Security Team ({{SECURITY_CONTACT}})

- Implement and operate security controls
- Monitor systems for security events and respond to incidents
- Conduct vulnerability assessments and penetration testing
- Manage security tooling and infrastructure

### 5.4 All Personnel

- Comply with all information security policies and procedures
- Report security incidents and suspicious activity promptly
- Complete required security awareness training
- Protect credentials and access tokens

## 6. Information Security Controls

### 6.1 Access Control

- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) required for all privileged access
- Least-privilege principle enforced across all systems
- Access reviews conducted quarterly
- Privileged access logged and monitored

### 6.2 Network Security

- Network segmentation between production, staging, and development environments
- Firewalls and intrusion detection/prevention systems deployed
- Encrypted communications required for all data in transit (TLS 1.2+)
- VPN or zero-trust network access for remote connections

### 6.3 Data Protection

- Encryption at rest for all sensitive data (AES-256 or equivalent)
- Data classification applied to all information assets
- Data loss prevention (DLP) controls implemented
- Secure disposal procedures for decommissioned media

### 6.4 Endpoint Security

- Endpoint detection and response (EDR) on all managed devices
- Automatic security updates and patch management
- Full-disk encryption required on all endpoints
- Mobile device management (MDM) for company-owned devices

### 6.5 Application Security

- Secure software development lifecycle (SSDLC) followed
- Static and dynamic application security testing (SAST/DAST)
- Dependency scanning and software composition analysis (SCA)
- Security review required before production deployment

### 6.6 Logging and Monitoring

- Centralized logging for all security-relevant events
- Security information and event management (SIEM) deployed
- Audit logs retained per data retention schedule
- Alerting configured for anomalous activity

## 7. Risk Management

- Annual risk assessments conducted to identify threats and vulnerabilities
- Risk register maintained and reviewed quarterly
- Risk treatment plans documented and tracked to completion
- Residual risk formally accepted by appropriate management level

## 8. Compliance

{{COMPANY_NAME}} maintains compliance with applicable regulatory requirements, including but not limited to:

- SOC 2 Type II
- ISO 27001
- GDPR (where applicable)
- Industry-specific regulations as required

## 9. Policy Violations

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or contract. Violations that constitute criminal offenses may be reported to law enforcement.

## 10. Review and Updates

This policy shall be reviewed at least annually or upon significant changes to the threat landscape, regulatory environment, or organizational structure. The next scheduled review is {{REVIEW_DATE}}.
