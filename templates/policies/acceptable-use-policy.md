## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the acceptable use of {{COMPANY_NAME}} information systems, networks, and resources. It protects {{COMPANY_NAME}} and its employees from the risks associated with misuse of technology assets.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, temporary workers, and third parties who access {{COMPANY_NAME}} information systems or networks, including company-owned and personal devices used for business purposes.

## 3. General Use

### 3.1 Ownership

All data created, stored, or transmitted on {{COMPANY_NAME}} systems remains the property of {{COMPANY_NAME}}. Users should have no expectation of privacy when using company resources.

### 3.2 Acceptable Use

{{COMPANY_NAME}} systems and resources are provided primarily for business use. Limited personal use is permitted provided it:

- Does not interfere with work responsibilities
- Does not consume excessive bandwidth or storage
- Does not violate any policy or law
- Does not expose {{COMPANY_NAME}} to security risk or liability

### 3.3 Monitoring

{{COMPANY_NAME}} reserves the right to monitor, log, and review all activity on its systems and networks for security, compliance, and operational purposes.

## 4. Prohibited Activities

The following activities are strictly prohibited:

### 4.1 Security Violations

- Attempting to bypass or disable security controls
- Sharing credentials or authentication tokens with others
- Connecting unauthorized devices to the corporate network
- Installing unauthorized software on company devices
- Intentionally introducing malware or malicious code
- Port scanning or network reconnaissance without authorization
- Exploiting system vulnerabilities without prior written approval

### 4.2 Data Handling Violations

- Transmitting confidential data through unapproved channels
- Storing sensitive data on personal cloud accounts
- Copying company data to unauthorized removable media
- Sharing customer data with unauthorized parties
- Disabling or circumventing data loss prevention controls

### 4.3 Communication Violations

- Sending harassing, threatening, or discriminatory messages
- Impersonating another person or entity
- Distributing chain letters, spam, or unsolicited mass communications
- Posting confidential information on social media or public forums
- Using company email for personal commercial purposes

### 4.4 Legal and Ethical Violations

- Accessing, downloading, or distributing illegal content
- Violating copyright, trademark, or licensing agreements
- Using company resources for unauthorized political activities
- Engaging in activities that create conflicts of interest

## 5. System and Network Use

### 5.1 Workstation Security

- Lock workstations when unattended (automatic lock within 5 minutes)
- Use full-disk encryption on all devices
- Keep operating systems and applications updated
- Run approved endpoint protection software
- Report lost or stolen devices to {{SECURITY_CONTACT}} immediately

### 5.2 Email and Messaging

- Exercise caution with attachments and links from unknown senders
- Use encryption for sensitive communications
- Do not auto-forward company email to external accounts
- Report suspicious emails to {{SECURITY_CONTACT}}
- Retain emails per the data retention schedule

### 5.3 Internet Use

- Access only work-related websites during business hours
- Do not download software without IT approval
- Use caution when accessing websites requiring personal information
- Do not use company network to access blocked or restricted sites via proxies

### 5.4 Cloud Services

- Use only approved cloud services for business data
- Do not create accounts on unapproved SaaS platforms using company email
- Enable MFA on all approved cloud services
- Follow data classification requirements for cloud storage

### 5.5 Remote Work

- Use VPN or approved zero-trust access for remote connections
- Ensure home network has adequate security (WPA3/WPA2, unique password)
- Do not use public Wi-Fi for accessing sensitive systems without VPN
- Secure physical workspace to prevent unauthorized viewing of screens

## 6. Software and Licensing

- Install only licensed and approved software
- Do not use pirated or counterfeit software
- Report any licensing compliance concerns to IT
- Open-source software must be reviewed and approved before use in production

## 7. Personal Devices (BYOD)

If {{COMPANY_NAME}} permits personal device use for business:

- Devices must meet minimum security requirements (encryption, PIN/biometric)
- Company data must be stored in managed containers
- {{COMPANY_NAME}} reserves the right to remotely wipe company data
- Device must be presented for inspection if a security incident is suspected
- Enrollment in mobile device management (MDM) may be required

## 8. Social Media

- Do not disclose confidential information on social media
- Clearly distinguish personal opinions from company positions
- Do not use company logos or branding without authorization
- Report any social engineering attempts to {{SECURITY_CONTACT}}

## 9. Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or contract. {{COMPANY_NAME}} may also pursue legal remedies for violations that result in harm.

This policy shall be reviewed at least annually. Next scheduled review: {{REVIEW_DATE}}.
